Dynamics of two-photon Tavis-Cummings model with Kerr media


Е. К. Bashkirov

Samara National Research University Samara, Russia bashkirov.ek@ssau.ru


Abstract—The entanglement between two qubits not- resonantly interacting with a single-mode field of an ideal resonator with Kerr media via degenerate two-photon transitions was studied. The exact solution for time-dependent density matrix and calculated on its basis the qubit-qubit entanglement parameter – negativity was derived. The results showed that Kerr media and detuning dramatically affected the entanglement behavior. More interestingly, that for initial entangled qubits states Kerr media and detuning avoids the entanglement sudden death effect.


Keywords—qubits, resonator, two-photon transitions, detuning, Kerr media, entanglement, negativity

  1. INTRODUCTION

    Entangled states are in the core of modern quantum informatics, since they are a physical resource on which many kinds of quantum technologies are based [1]. Hence, it is of crucial importance not only to understand entanglement properties, but also to manipulate entanglement states in a practical way. Thus, the operation and control of the evolution of qubits is one of the main directions for realizing quantum computing and quantum information processing [2-4]. Quantum computing and quantum communication require maximally entangled qubit-qubit stable states with large times of decoherence. Qubit-qubit entanglement can be produced by means of the interaction of natural and artificial atoms (Rydberg atoms and ions in traps, spins, quantum dots, superconducting rings with Josephson junctions, hybrid and optomechanical systems) with the electromagnetic field of the resonator [2]. In quantum world we can’t neglect the interaction between qubits in quantum devices and environment. It is well known that this interaction usually reduce the amount of qubits entanglement. However, in some cases, interaction with the environment may, on the contrary, enhance the entanglement [5]. In particular, qubits entanglement may be induced by a thermal noise. This effect as applied to a system of two two-level atoms with single-photon transitions in a resonator with a thermal field was predicted by P. Knight et al. [6].Thermal noise-induced entanglement in a two-atom system with two-photon transitions was investigated in [7-10].     Thus, the study of mechanisms that prevent the sudden death of qubit-qubit entanglement becomes one of the priority tasks of quantum informatics. Quantum devices using qubits of various physical nature as logic elements operate in a wide temperature range from mK (superconducting qubits) to room temperatures (NV centers). This means the presence of thermal photons in the resonators of such quantum devices. Therefore, it is of considerable interest to study the mechanisms that prevent the sudden death of entanglement between qubits interacting with the thermal fields of resonators. In this paper, we study the role of the Kerr nonlinearity in the dynamics of qubits interacting with the thermal noise of a resonator.

    The Kerr effect has been successfully used to produce different nonlinear optic phenomena. Recently, a large number of papers have been devoted to studying the dynamics of qubits in resonators with a Kerr medium [13- 17]. Kerr nonlinearity in atomic systems are small compared to atom-field coupling and photon loss parameter, therefore the Kerr medium slightly affects the dynamics of the atoms interacting with electromagnetic waves. At the same time, as was shown experimentally, for superconducting qubits in coplanar resonators, it was possible to realize a regime in which the Kerr medium should significantly affect the dynamics of qubits [18].

    In this paper we considered the dynamics of two initially entangled qubits with two-photon transitions interacting with a thermal electromagnetic field of a resonator in the framework of generalized Tavis-Cummins model [19] taking into account the Kerr nonlinearity.

In Figs. 1-2 we present the results of numerical

Fig. 1 demonstrates the time dependence of the negativity for initial qubits state (2) and various average photon numbers, detunings and Kerr nonlinearities. As can be seen from Fig. 1, as the detuning or Kerr nonlinearity increases, the maximum amount of entanglement also greatly increases. Let us note that for model with two-photon interaction, the increase in negativity is much greater than that for the model with one-photon interaction. The time dependences of negativity also demonstrate that for resonant qubits-field interaction in the resonator without Kerr media, the effect of sudden death of entanglement occurs. However, this effect can easily be eliminated by including the Kerr nonlinearity or slight detuning. Such an action can easily be performed for qubits, in particular for superconducting qubits, in which the energy gap between the ground and excited level can easily change with a change in the magnetic field.

quantum Liouville equation for the statistical operator of the model under considerations in the “dressed” states representation was derived. On its basis the qubit-qubit entanglement criterion – negativity was calculated. The results showed that for separable initial qubits state the Kerr nonlinearity and detuning can greatly enhances the maximal degree of entanglement. For entangled initial qubits states Kerr nonlinearity and detuning smoothes negativity amplitude fluctuations induced by a thermal resonator field. In this case the effect of sudden death of entanglement can dies away for thermal initial resonator state.
